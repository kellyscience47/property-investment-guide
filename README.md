# Taiwan Property Investment Tax Guide 2026
## The Complete Cheat Sheet for Smart Property Investors

---

## 1. Capital Gains Tax (CGT) 2.0 — 房地合一稅

Taiwan's property CGT uses a **cliff-based rate system** tied to holding period:

| Holding Period | Tax Rate | Strategy |
|---------------|----------|----------|
| ≤ 2 years | **45%** | Avoid selling — wait for next cliff |
| 2-5 years | **35%** | Consider holding to 5-year mark |
| 5-10 years | **20%** | Sweet spot for most investors |
| > 10 years | **15%** | Lowest possible rate |

### 🔑 Key Insight
The jump from 45% → 35% happens at exactly **730 days**. If you're at day 700, waiting 30 more days saves you **10% of your entire capital gain**. On a NT$2M gain, that's NT$200,000 saved.

### Self-Use Exemption (自住優惠)
If you meet ALL three criteria, your rate drops to **10%** with a NT$4M exemption:
- ✅ Registered household (設籍) at the property for 6+ years
- ✅ No rental income or business use during ownership
- ✅ Not used this exemption in the past 6 years

## 2. Hoarding Tax (囤房稅 2.0) — Effective July 2024

Multiple property owners pay **escalating property tax rates**:

| Properties Owned | Self-Use Rate | Non-Self-Use Rate |
|-----------------|---------------|-------------------|
| 1 (self-use) | 1.0% | — |
| 2-3 non-self-use | — | 2.0% |
| 4-5 non-self-use | — | 3.6% |
| 6+ non-self-use | — | 4.8% |

⚠️ **Nationwide count** — properties across ALL cities count toward your total.

### Tax-Saving Strategy
- Register as self-use (自住) for up to 3 properties
- Rent out properties to qualify for rental income deductions
- Consider transferring to family members (but watch gift tax!)

## 3. Deductible Expenses for Landlords

### Standard Deduction Method (簡易法)
- Deduct **43% of rental income** automatically
- No receipts needed
- Best for landlords with low actual expenses

### Itemized Deduction Method (列舉法)
Deduct actual expenses:
- ✅ Mortgage interest
- ✅ Building management fee (管理費)
- ✅ Fire/earthquake insurance
- ✅ Repair & maintenance costs
- ✅ Depreciation (straight-line method)
- ✅ Land value tax (地價稅)
- ✅ Property inspection fees
- ❌ Principal repayment (NOT deductible)
- ❌ Renovation for personal preference

### Which Method?
If actual expenses > 43% of rental income → use itemized
If actual expenses < 43% of rental income → use standard

## 4. NHI Supplementary Premium (二代健保)

Rental income above **NT$20,000/month** triggers a **2.11%** surcharge.

| Monthly Rent | Annual NHI Premium |
|-------------|-------------------|
| NT$20,000 | NT$0 (below threshold) |
| NT$30,000 | NT$2,532 |
| NT$50,000 | NT$7,596 |
| NT$80,000 | NT$15,192 |

## 5. Land Value Tax (地價稅) vs House Tax (房屋稅)

| Tax | Base | Rate | Due |
|-----|------|------|-----|
| Land Value Tax | Government-assessed land value | 0.2% (self-use) to 5.5% | November |
| House Tax | Government-assessed building value | 1.0-4.8% | May |

### Self-Use Land Tax Rate
Apply for the preferential **0.2%** rate (vs general 1%) if:
- You or spouse/dependents are registered at the address
- Only one property per person qualifies

## 6. Repurchase Tax Refund (重購退稅)

Sell your home and buy another? Get your CGT **refunded**!

### Eligibility
- ✅ Both properties must be self-use
- ✅ Buy within 2 years of selling (before or after)
- ✅ No rental/business use on either property

### Refund Amount
- New purchase price ≥ sale price → **Full refund**
- New purchase price < sale price → **Proportional refund**

## 7. Gift & Inheritance Tax Quick Reference

### Gift Tax (贈與稅)
- Annual exemption: **NT$2.44M** per donor
- Rates: 10% / 15% / 20% (progressive)
- Spouse transfers: **Exempt**

### Inheritance Tax (遺產稅)
- Basic exemption: **NT$13.33M**
- Spouse deduction: NT$5.53M
- Per-child deduction: NT$560K
- Rates: 10% / 15% / 20%

### Pro Tip
Gradual gifting over multiple years (staying under NT$2.44M/year) can transfer significant property value tax-free.

## 8. Annual Tax Filing Checklist for Landlords

### May Filing Season (Every Year)
- [ ] Calculate rental income from all properties
- [ ] Choose standard (43%) or itemized deduction
- [ ] Gather receipts if using itemized method
- [ ] Report NHI supplementary premium
- [ ] Declare any property sales (CGT)
- [ ] Check depreciation schedule
- [ ] File house tax (due May)
- [ ] Review assessed values for accuracy

### November
- [ ] Pay land value tax
- [ ] Apply for self-use preferential rate if eligible
